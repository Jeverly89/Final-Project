    AQ by State

    Higher AQI values indicate poorer air quality. The US air quality in general is rated very moderately. The air we breathe is not life-threatening, but it can impact sensitive groups, especially in states with higher values. Hawaii is a considerable positive outlier, likely due to its island/coastal nature and lack of urbanization, which help allow natural airflow. AQI between 0–50 is considered good and 51–100 is moderate. The point of this graph is not to show that the US has overall good air quality, but to show the differences between states and regions within the country.
