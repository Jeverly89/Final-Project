    • States in the Northeast and West show the highest life expectancy like Hawaii, California, Massachusetts, and Washington. These states have life expectancy of 80 years or more.
    • Many Southern and Appalachian states such as Mississippi, Alabama, Louisiana, Arkansas, and West Virginia have significantly lower life expectancy, at around 77 or fewer.
    • The map reveals a clear North–South divide, with northern and western states consistently outperforming the Deep South.
    • States with high infrastructure and more urban areas tend to have higher life expectancy compared to more rural states.
    • Women consistently live longer than men across the country, with a higher median life expectancy. Women also tend to have less variability in life expectancy, suggesting more stable outcomes.
    • Men have a wider spread of life expectancy values, showing greater potential for high life expectancy but also more risk for low values.
    • The boxplot clearly visualizes the gender gap in life expectancy, which we do not suspect to be abnormal.
    • Public health data shows that there are biological or behavioral differences contributing to women living longer than men on average.
